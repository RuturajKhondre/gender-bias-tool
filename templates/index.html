Examples of Good vs. Biased Writing

Example 1:

Biased: "The chairman called all businessmen to the meeting."

Inclusive: "The chairperson called all business people to the meeting."

Example 2:

Biased: "Each student should hand in his assignment."

Inclusive: "Students should hand in their assignments."

Example 3:

Biased: "The lady doctor performed the surgery."

Inclusive: "The doctor performed the surgery."

Learn More About Gender Bias

Gender bias in language can perpetuate stereotypes and inequality. It's important to be aware of how our words can impact others.

Common Types of Gender Bias in Language:

  • Using male-centric terms as default (e.g., "mankind" instead of "humanity")
  • Gendered job titles (e.g., "policeman" instead of "police officer")
  • Stereotypical descriptions (e.g., "bossy" for assertive women, "emotional" for men showing feelings)

By using inclusive language, we can create a more equitable environment for all genders.
